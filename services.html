Financial Management

This can be defined as the management of the finances of an entity in order to achieve the financial objectives.

Key objectives of financial management would be to:

  • Create wealth for the business
  • Generate cash
  • Provide an adequate return on investment bearing in mind the risks that the business is taking and the resources invested.

Areas of service would include:

  1. CASH FLOW BUDGETING AND MANAGEMENT – A cash budget details a company’s cash inflow and outflow during a specified budget period, such as a month, quarter or year. Its primary purpose is to provide the status of the company’s cash position at any point of time. This helps the company make critical decisions such as creating cash reserves to make arrangements for projected shortages and using excess funds prudently. Additionally, the cash budget helps in prioritizing payments in the budget period. It also helps in analysing budget-versus-actual variances in cash inflow and outflow.

  2. ACCOUNTING SUPPORT – Your accounting system is analysed and proper on the job training is given to your accountant in all aspects of best practice financial management and financial reporting standards.

  3. INVESTMENT & FUNDING READINESS PREPARATION – In order to qualify for outside funding, your books, financials and internal controls needs to be in place and in order. We can identify shortfalls in these areas and recommend solutions for this. Financial modelling, valuations, due diligence and more come into play as you prepare for closing that fundraising round.

  4. RISK MITIGATION & INTERNAL CONTROLS – Risk is an inherent part of doing business. The purpose of risk management and internal control is to ensure that the company’s operations are effective, that financial and other information is reliable, and that the company complies with the relevant regulations and operating principles In order to identify and manage business risks, we conduct detailed analysis of your business cycle and operating environment. Thereafter together with you, standard operating procedures and a control environment where risk is minimised while output is optimised can be created.

  5. POST-INVESTMENT FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT – Where venture capital and private equity companies are concerned, we can support you by coming in as a post-investment financial manager. Where you as an investor have bought into a private company, but are not directly involved with business management, we can to into the business to actively monitor and report on financial decision-making and performance and to add value to the growth of the company.

  6. FUNDRAISING SERVICES – We can assist you in raising funds for your company by approaching banks, venture capitalist and private equity investors on your behalf and compile documents that they require to look at your proposal. Arranging meetings with investors or their agents. Facilitate meetings between relevant parties, during and up to completion of the deal.

  7. IMPLEMENTATION OF MANAGEMENT ACCOUNTS – We can identify and format the type of management accounts your directors/holding company requires on a monthly basis for management and board meetings. Areas of risk can be identified, goals met can be determined and shortcoming analysed. Cost saving measures and additional revenue generation areas can be identified.

  8. STRATEGY & SYNERGIES – We can assist in the development of the business strategy, Translating the strategy, Aligning the organization to the business strategy, Planning the operations of the business to work towards the strategy, Developing a monitoring and learning framework for the organization, Testing and adapting the strategy with the changing internal and external environments.

  9. BUSINESS ANALYSIS & COST STRUCTURING – As business analysts, we identify and define the solutions that will maximize the value delivered by an organization to its stakeholders. All fixed and variable costs are reviewed to help your business run at maximum capacity.

  10. FINANCIAL DECISION-MAKING – Determining the best method for investments i.e. via borrowing from bank, selling new shares, or taking credit from suppliers. Determining whether profits earned should be retained rather than distributed to shareholders via dividends. If dividends are too high, the entity may be starved of funding to reinvest in growing revenues and profits further

  11. AD HOC ENGAGEMENTS – Additional services such as:
  12. Compilation of financial statements
  13. Audit readiness engagements
  14. Financial plan set-up and review
  15. Financial due diligence
  16. Business Valuations
  17. Drawing up of Budgets
  18. Drafting of Financials as per auditors requirements

Rent a CFO

By renting one, which can range from a few days a week or more, can provide flexibility, in which you are not locked into a salaried hire, if the CFO you try the first time is not the perfect fit, there is less risk involved selecting a different consultant.
An outsourced or interim CFO can work very closely with your management team on your company’s financial strategy and conduct an analysis of your current operations financial processes. And when an owner, MD or CEO collaborates with a financial expert at this level, you can work together to implement initiatives designed to increase profits and reduce costs for both the long and short term.

There are a number of specific duties that a CFO can perform and do for a company:

  • * Identifying strategic goals to drive profits and other strategic initiatives
  • * Oversee financial and accounting functions and train staff on accounting best practices and technologies.
  • * Assist with taxes and adhering to tax laws to minimize overall tax liability as well as potential interest and penalties
  • * Evaluate your cost and pricing structure to strengthen gross margins
  • * Upgrade your financial reporting system, including re-designing reports for clarity
  • * Preparing financial information used in internal or external reporting, including collaborating with the company’s existing accountant
  • * Assessing financial consequences of proposed transactions
  • * Evaluate the internal controls and help initiate protocols to reduce the risk of theft and fraud
  • * Setting up the appropriate infrastructure for financial metrics, which can be crucial for the management in order for them to make the best decisions for the company moving forward
  • * Help to lower labour costs while providing expert strategic financial direction.
  • * Can also serve as the business liaison with potential lenders, investors and other outside financing sources
  • * Preparing and monitoring budgets and forecasts, including cash forecasting
  • * Assessing profit margins lining up with industry expectations
  • * Evaluating and developing inventory tracking systems
  • * Assessing IT and HR needs
  • * Develop cash improvement programs
  • * Developing compensation plans for key employees
  • * Evaluating internal accounting controls

Step 1: Information gathering

We work as a team with you to finalize your business plan. That means we need to understand your business and your intentions for future development. We gather this information by having detailed discussion with you.

Step 2: Preparation of plan

As you know your business best, we believe this is the ideal way to develop your plan. We utilize the information you provide to fill in the gaps and develop the tailor made plan for your business venture.

Step 3: Completion

We will make any clarifications, alterations and corrections for a period of 30 days after the delivery of the initial completed plan.
